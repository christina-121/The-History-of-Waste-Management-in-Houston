Galveston Bay is the economic and ecological heart of the region. It provides seafood, recreation, and habitat. But it's also the receiving body for Houston's watershed. Rain carries litter, chemicals, and debris from streets and bayous directly into the bay. Key efforts today include:

  • Bayou trash collectors: "Trash traps" in Buffalo and White Oak Bayous capture floating debris.
  • Stormwater permits: Require industries and cities to reduce polluted runoff.
  • Plastic bag ordinances: Some local bans to reduce singleâ€‘use plastics.
  • Public education: "Don't Mess with Texas" and "Galveston Bay Foundation" campaigns.
